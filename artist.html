Frida Kahlo

Magdalena Carmen Frida Kahlo Calderón, was born in Coyoacán, Mexico City. At 18 years old, she Frida had a tragic accident. The bus she was riding in was hit by a tram. and with this she suffered an accident that left her immobile. Due to the immobility to which she was subjected for several months, she Frida began to paint. Thus, she is related to several artists, among them the photographer Tina Modotti and the then well-known artist Diego Rivera; who would later become her husband. Frida's works are mostly self-portraits and generally speak of Mexican culture and suffering.

Diego Rivera

.Diego María de la Concepción Juan Nepomuceno Estanislao de la Rivera y Barrientos Acosta y Rodríguez was a realist painter, cubist and Mexican muralist. Born in Guanajuato, he was famous for capturing works of high political and social content in public buildings. Diego's work and his wife, the painter Frida Kahlo, influenced each other. He was the creator of various murals in different parts of the now called Historic Center of Mexico City, as well as in the National School of Agriculture of Chapingo, and in other Mexican, North American and South American cities..

Jose Guadalupe Posada

Jose Guadalupe Posada Aguilar, born in Aguascalientes, was a Mexican engraver, illustrator and cartoonist. Famous for his drawings of traditional scenes, folklore, socio-political criticism and for his illustrations of "calacas" or skulls, the best known of his illustrations is "La Catrina", a curious fact about this work is that La Catrina does not have clothes, only hat. For Posada it meant a criticism of Mexicans who are poor, but want to appear to have a higher standard of living.

Jose Clemente Orozco

Jose Clemente Orozco Flores was born in Zapotlán, Jalisco (Today Ciudad Guzmán), he was a Mexican cartoonist, muralist and lithographer. Graduated from the National School of Agriculture, he also studied mathematics and architectural drawing. The greatest works of him are in the Hospicio Cabañas, the most interesting thing is that many of his works were that he did only with one hand, since two poorly attended wounds in the left hand caused gangrene and the consequent amputation of the limb up to the wrist.
